FROM COLLECTORS TO CREATORS

Using found footage from cobblers and sneaker redesigners, a documentary offers a glimpse into an alternative world where the importance of newness is replaced with heritage, disposability with repairability, and mass production with craft. KEY QUESTION? How might we shift the values of newness in fashion?

SCAN

The investigation began with a look into the future of sports. After thorough and broad research, the future of athletic equipment and apparel revealed itself as not only consequential to our ability to train and play, but also a core aspect of fashion and sustainability. Through using frameworks like STEEP and AEIOU, I dove deeper into weak signals that stood out through the noise. It was clear that the fashion industry was shifting to address its effects on climate, and evolving consumer mindsets. Evidence suggested new ways of buying, approaches to raw materials, and overall, a need to change our relationship to clothing as we know it. One question stood out, how would brands make refurbish or repaired clothing more attractive than something new?

ZOOM

To understand my question more thoroughly I interviewed Rory Fortune, a modern day cobbler that runs a shop called Goods and Services, and Kempe Scanlan, a fashion designer, trends consultant, and futurist. Conversations brought to light the intricacies of how the fashion landscape was changing, both through Scanlan’s experience as a trends consultant and through Fortune’s first hand experience as a shop owner. They also revealed the possibilities of the digital fashion space, which on the surface felt like a counterpoint to repaired and refurbished physical fashion, but in reality hinted at opportunities about how the digital and physical could support one another.

DEVELOP

Based on my research I decided to focus on sneakers because of its importance to iconic brands like Nike, its evolution from something repairable to disposable, and the fanatic culture that surrounds it. I began developing a story to illustrate the culture a new culture around sneakers, centering around the people that make the shoes, the stories and connections we can make with clothing and how we could extend them through repair, and touching on how a digital footwear market could aid this shift. As a vehicle for the story, I started edited a short documentary using found footage to immerse my audience into an alternative world where culture around fashion has fundamentally changed.

DISCUSS

The resulting short film hints at a world where sustainable fashion is very much within reach through revitalizing tools, our relationship with artisans, and retooling our relationship with clothing. It suggests how a brand like Nike could participate in this process, and how people could relate with clothing differently.
